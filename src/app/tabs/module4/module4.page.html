Every Drop Counts

While water covers most of Earth's surface, only a tiny portion, about 10%, is freshwater readily available for drinking, sanitation, and other human uses. The rest is saline (saltwater) or locked away in glaciers and ice caps.

 Where Does Our Water Go?

Many people assume showers are the biggest water guzzlers. However, flushing the toilet often takes the top spot. Be mindful of leaks and consider installing low-flush toilets to conserve this precious resource.

 Sustainable Water Management

Sustainable water management involves using water wisely and finding alternative sources. Collecting rainwater for watering your garden is a fantastic example. This reduces reliance on treated tap water and puts rainwater to good use.

 Water Pollution - A Threat to Our Treasure

Water pollution is a serious threat to aquatic ecosystems and human health. Industrial waste, littering, and runoff from agricultural land containing pesticides and fertilizers are all major sources of water pollution.

 Be a Water Wise Warrior - Every Action Counts!

Water conservation is a collective effort. Taking shorter showers, fixing leaky faucets, and turning off the faucet while brushing your teeth are all easy ways to be a Water Wise Warrior and contribute to a sustainable future!
